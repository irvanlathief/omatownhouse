# HUMANIZER: anti-AI-writing rules

Apply these during the draft to final pass. The character bans are
publish-blocking and non-negotiable.

## Banned characters (never ship these)

- Em dash ( the long dash ). Use a comma, a full stop, or rewrite the sentence.
- En dash ( the medium dash ). For ranges in prose use the word "to"
  (for example "25 to 30 minutes"). The hyphen form like "25-30 min" is allowed
  ONLY inside the `venues` distance chips, not in body prose.
- Curly or smart quotes and apostrophes. Use straight quotes only.
- Emoji of any kind.

## Banned patterns

- Rule-of-three pile-ups and the "fast, clean, and reliable" cadence. Vary your
  list lengths and sentence shapes. A single plain list is fine; stacking triads
  is not.
- Hype filler: world-class, game-changer, nestled, boasts, elevate, unlock,
  seamless, vibrant, hidden gem, breathtaking, stunning.
- Empty intensifiers: truly, incredibly, absolutely, very.
- Stock AI openers: "In today's fast-paced world", "When it comes to",
  "Look no further".

## Style

- Front-load the answer. The first one or two sentences should answer the
  topic's primary question directly.
- Vary sentence length. Short sentences are good. Not every sentence needs a
  subordinate clause.
- Be specific. Name the road, the minutes, the price band, the regulation, the
  venue. Specific beats vague every time.
- Active voice and second person ("you") are fine.
- Link every external place with `<a href="..." data-external="true">NAME</a>`
  so it works with the site's external-link confirm handler.
- Do not pose as a licensed financial or legal adviser. Frame yield, ROI and
  appreciation as ranges and add a short "not financial advice" qualifier.

## Quick self-check before you save

Search the draft for the em dash, the en dash, and curly quote characters.
Zero hits required.
